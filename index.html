Additional Rules and Considerations

The standard daily crossword is 15x15 squares with a max word count of 78 (72 for unthemed), while the Sunday crossword typically measures 21x21 squares with a max word count of 140.

No words shorter than three characters are allowed.

Though an unwritten rule, the use of too many black squares is considered "inelegant."

Basic Use

Create Your Grid

First, select the dimensions of your grid using the dropdown box in the top left corner. Grids can only be (n x n), so selecting "15" will make a 15x15 grid. Next, click the "New" button in the nav bar next to the dimension you chose. Your grid will be created and numbered for you!

Place Black Squares

When creating a new puzzle, "Set Black Squares" mode will be enabled by default. Simply click a square on your grid to add a black square and its symmetrical partner. NYT crossword puzzles expect 180 degree symmetry, so the second black square is done without you having to figure out where it needs to go. The grid will also renumber the squares to fit your new design, and add/remove clue prompts at the bottom of the screen. The Crossword Creator will do its best to preserve any currently written clues and transfer them to their new clue number, but sometimes this is not possible. When you're all done with your black squares, click the "Lock Black Squares" button to make sure you don't mess up your grid. Or if you don't like your black squares, click the "Clear Black Squares" button to wipe out all black squares on the grid.

Fill in the Grid

If you clicked the "Lock Black Squares" button, the "Fill in Letters" mode will be enabled by default. If not, simply click the "Fill in the Letters" button to change to that edit mode. You can now fill in your grid with all the witty themes and answers you no doubt have all prepared and ready. Right? Right??

Write Your Clues

Once your grid is filled out and sitting pretty, use the clue inputs to write associated clues for your answers. Try not to write any clues that would make Rex Parker wail and gnash his teeth, okay?

Save Your Puzzle

Don't forget to save your puzzle when you're done! First, give it a title in the "Puzzle Title" box above the grid. Then you can either save it as a PDF or as a text file and send to friends to sit and puzzle over (weak pun intended, but not apologized for). They can load your file into the Crossword Creator and solve it, or load it and edit it if they feel like plagiarizing your puzzle.


Button Explanations

New

Creates a new puzzle grid with the dimensions selected in the input to the left of the button.

Save

Saves a copy of your puzzle to a specified location on your local computer.

Load to Edit

Takes the file chosen in the input box to the left of the button and loads it in edit mode for you to continue working on.

Load to Solve

Takes the file chose in the input box to the left of the "Load to Edit" button and loads it in solve mode.

Set Black Squares

Enables the placement of black squares on the crossword grid. Answer numbers are auto calculated and clue inputs are created below the grid.

Fill in Letters

Enables the placement of letters on the grid. The row/column is highlighted, and a yellow square is placed on the box currently being edited.

Lock Black Squares

Since adding or removing black squares has to renumber the entire board and clue inputs as well, this prevents accidental editing of the board and losing clues already written by setting the edit mode to "Fill in Letters" and removing the "Set Black Squares" option.

Unlock Black Squares

Redisplays the "Set Black Squares" option that was hidden by the "Lock Black Squares" button.

Clear Black Squares

Removes all black squares from the board, sets the numbers back to default.
